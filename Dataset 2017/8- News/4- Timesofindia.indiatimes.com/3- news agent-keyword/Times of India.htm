        • Travel agents must promote tourism in Kashmir
          1 sec ago

          Nagpur: Kashmir is the safest place for tourists in the world but many are under the impression that it is not. Everyone must come and see for themselves only then the general perception will change, said Ibrahim Siah, president of Travel Agents Society of Kashmir (TASK) while speaking at a seminar here on Friday. The objective was to create awareness about the growing tourism industry in Jammu & Kashmir among travel agents in the city. Director of floriculture, garden and parks (Government of J & K) Mohammad Hussain Mir, chairman of Travel Agents Association of India (TAAI), Nagpur chapter Jatindra Nakade and president of Travel Agents Association of Nagpur Sanjay Deshpande addressed the audience. Deshpande said people who approach us with their travel plans to Kashmir often express concern over safety. As travel agents, we must promote tourism in Kashmir and make people realize it is truly a safe place, he said. In a brief discussion with Mir, Deshpande highlighted issues like time restrictions for being indoors and tourist being charged an unreasonable amount for particular services. Mir promised that the the issues will be addressed. According to Mir, tourism in Kashmir plummeted to an all-time low in 1989 owing to the ethnic cleansing of Kashmiri Hindus. The industry is now picking pace. Around 12 lakh tourists visited the region and over 1 crore visited the state in June last year, he said. J&K is now promoting different branches of tourism like hospitality, sports and adventure tourism, said Mir. He also informed about marriage tourism and invited people to organize wedding ceremonies in Kashmir. Mir claimed that tourism is currently contributing to 60% of the states economy and other industries, especially small and medium enterprises, rely on it heavily. If tourism dies, the economy of the state will crash, said Mir. TAAI is working towards tourism development in India and J & K is our priority. This will greatly benefit locals involved in handicraft and other businesses, said Nakade while addressing travel agents.

        • AIR Malayalam news bulletin moves out of national capital
          28 Jan 2017

          KANNUR: The Malayalam news bulletin which is being broadcast from the AIR studios in New Delhi now will soon be aired from the state capital. This is part of the Prasar Bharatis decision to shift the national language bulletins from the headquarters to the respective state capital regional news units. Besides Malayalam, Tamil, Odiya and Assamese news bulletins will be aired from the respective state capitals from March 1, said a circular issued by Prasar Bharathi. Though the shifting might not affect its national angle, as the news would be prepared from Delhi, the nostalgic connection with the place is something that would be missing now, said Gopan, former newsreader and the man behind give voice to the anti-tobacco campaign with the catchline swasakosam sponge poleyanu! It was on January 1, 1949 the first Malayalam news bulletin was aired from the Delhi studio of AIR, which remained the common Malayalis window to the world for years. The first news bulletin was read out by K Padmanabhan. In the years to come, Malayalis became familiar with many of those voices behind the bullettins. It is true that over the years, the quality of the bulletin has deteriorated and there is nothing wrong in shifting it. But we should not forget this was the bulletin that was so attached to the culture of the Malayalis and we all became household names in Kerala through it, said Gopan. "When the bulletin from Delhi goes off air, a piece of history is falling into oblivion," opined T N Sushama, a former newsreader with the AIR. It was literally through these bulletins that the Keralites came closer to the national happenings, and there were instances when we broke even the news happenings in Kerala. But now the newsroom has lost its charm, though we have some excellent news presenters there, she added. Though he carries a lot of nostalgic memories associated with the news unit, and agrees that the quality has deteriorated now, Rosscote Krishna Pillai, one of the earliest newsreaders in the AIR Malayalam unit in Delhi, is of the opinion that it was meaningless to shift it. That news bulletin has a history that is even older than the news broadcasting in Kerala, and that broadcasting quality cannot be maintained if it is shifted to Kerala, he said. It was also a platform for the people abroad to listen to the bulletin in their mother tongue, and if it is shifted to Kerala, it will no longer have that charm, said Pillai, who began his association with the unit in 1951. The transmitters in Kerala did not have the capacity of the ones in Delhi, and if shifted it would become a local bulletin, he opined, adding that there should be a campaign to retain it there and improve the quality.

        • Paid news under Election Commission's scanner
          28 Jan 2017

          CHANDIGARH: Punjab chief electoral officer (CEO) has issued notices to Punjab chief minister Parkash Singh Badal, deputy CM Sukhbir Badal and Indian Youth Congress (IYC) president Amrinder Singh Raja Warring for paid advertisements in the media. Addressing a press conference, additional CEO C Sibin said the politicians identified for paid news will have to add it to their campaign expenditure. They can, however, go into appeal against the notice. The case of chief minister Badal and deputy CM Sukhbir Badal is under process, as the notice was issued on January 26. We have received 50 complaints, out of which 19 notices have been issued. Seven were found unpaid news and 24 complaints are under process. Notices will be issued after verifying these complaints, he said. The Election Commission has served notices on 19 candidates, which also includes the names of Congress candidates Darshan Lal from Balachaur (in Nawanshahar) and SAD candidate Jagdeep Singh Nakai from Joga constituently (in Moga). Darshan, Warring and Jagdeep are repeat offenders as they have five complaints each on paid news. As per the ECI guidelines there is no provision to disclose the name of the media house. The expense of paid news will be added to the expenditure of all 19 candidates. Paid news is an offence with two years of imprisonment, if ECI found them guilty, though these candidates can appeal before the ECI, said Sibin. A total of 1,061 out of 1,145 candidates in fray have been on EC radar after complaints against them. The complaints include excess flow of cash, drugs and violation of code of conduct among others. Till date, the EC has recovered drugs including heroine, poppy husk, and opium worth Rs 12 crore, Rs 5.90 crore in cash, gold worth Rs 26 crore and liquor worth Rs 2.5 crore. The EC has recovered 95% weapons under arms and licence act provisions. Meanwhile, 31,460 electronic voter machines (EVMs) and 33 Voter Verifiable Paper Audit Trail (VVPAT) machines will be installed at the district headquarters and key constituencies.
